Kana

Kana (仮名) are Japanese syllabic scripts.

Each character of the kana corresponds to a sound following either a consonant-vowel pattern or a single vowel pattern. There is one exception to this: the sound most similar to a Western N sound.

Traditional Japanese text is presented in gojuon order: top-down, right to left. This will be the method used for the following charts.

There are also several blank tiles in the charts. For these blanks, there are no provided written or spoken sounds - either they do not exist, or they have been excluded due to modern usage. There are also a few sounds that are vocalized slightly differently than the normal pattern would suggest; take note of these special cases.

Dakuten and Handakuten

Dakuten (濁点) characters are variants of standard kana characters used to indicated that the consonant of the character should be voiced in its alternative form.

Handakuten (半濁点) are very similar to Dakuten, with the exception that they are applied only to the H consonant stemmed characters to form a P sound.

Youon

Youon (拗音), or yōon, characters are special patterns in which an I tailed character is combined with a Y consonant stemmed character.

In hiragana, as opposed to katakana, the Y-characters are written with a slightly smaller script. While this change is subtle, in cases where only context would lend to the distinction between homonyms, the written form indicates the intended word.
